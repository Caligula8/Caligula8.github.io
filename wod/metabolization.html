Terence Mckenna was an American ethnobotanist, lecturer, writer, philosopher, and psychonaut who’s life goal was to promote the responsible use of naturally occurring plant based hallucinogens. In his interviews, lectures, and books, the topics he discussed ranged across psychedelic drugs, plant-based entheogens, shamanism, metaphysics, alchemy, language, philosophy, culture, technology, environmentalism, and the theoretical origins of human consciousness. Many of his beliefs may seem crazy at first such as his Timewave-Zero theory or the claim to the existence of machine elves, but his conceptual imagination in language so potent and eloquent that within the first hour of listening to him speak, he almost always completely shifts or transcends the listeners reality from what it previously was, regardless of the lecture. He has the most extraordinary aptitude to describe nature and subjective experiences under the influence of hallucinogens in the most extraordinarily lucid detail.

Note that my love for the ideas and work of Terence Mckenna does not mean that I condone the use of any illegal substance in areas in which they are illegal, I very much enjoy the perks of being a law abiding citizen and do not plan to even go near any psychedelic substance until much later in my life (30-40 years old). At that point my options would be (assuming the laws regarding drug legality stay the same in which case they won’t) either therapeutic Ayahuasca session(s) under the influence of a Shaman or Psilocybin truffles in the Netherlands which are completely legal.

Terence mckenna is however the reason I wrote this essay; the issues he raised against society and culture were very legitimate and ever since, even before I thought about writing this essay, I have been researching every possible resource I could find to better educate myself on the subject.
